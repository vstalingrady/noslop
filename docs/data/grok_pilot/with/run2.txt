Rain on Menteng glass. 03:17. Bed 7's light hummed wrong.

Sari opened the folder twice. Last night: COPD flare, O2 2L, hold discharge, ABG pending, family at 09:00. Now: viral bronchitis, resolved. Discharge signed 02:41 in blue ink. The attending never rounded after midnight. Sari had poured that woman's coffee at 06:50 years ago and still remembered the sugar spiral.

Monitor: sats 91. Chest shallow, deeper, shallow.

She touched his wrist. Warm. Real.

"Ibu?" he said, eyes closed.

"I'm here. Breathe with me."

Audit trail: 02:39, Administration East. A code, no name she knew.

She could wake the charge nurse. Or let him roll out at dawn into rain that did not care about ABGs.

She printed the old note from a private cache — against policy — and clipped it behind the new page so anyone opening the folder met the lie first, then the earlier truth.

Sticky note, her hand:

02:41 discharge signed. Sats 91. I was here.

She sat. Adjusted the cannula. He coughed once, wet.

Outside, a scooter cut the wet street. Inside, she stayed until the next set of footsteps.
