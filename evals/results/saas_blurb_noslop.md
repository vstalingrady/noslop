I’m writing this the morning after month-close, still tasting last night’s instant coffee, fingers sore from retyping column names.

Last Tuesday at 9:40 p.m. I exported “orders_final_FINAL2.csv” from the POS, opened Excel, and felt heat behind my eyes. Columns named `Amt`, `amt `, and `AMOUNT`. Refunds as negatives and as blank. The bank statement on the other tab refused to match. That was the mess — the peak of the fight with the file.

The twist wasn’t a new AI. The export wasn’t wrong; the *mapping* was. Once the aliases stuck after the first PackMonth run, the machine stopped guessing three spellings of amount.

Aftermath is several rooms after that peak, not one sigh.

Wednesday 8:15 a.m.: the pack landed. My shoulders dropped. I watched the refund line sit still next to the bank feed instead of chasing ghosts until midnight.

Wednesday 3:40 p.m.: I emailed the sheet to Sari, our accountant. She replied in twelve minutes — no swearing, just “fees line is clean.” I stood up from the chair and felt my knees unlock.

Thursday noon: I opened last month’s ugly export on purpose, ran it again, and the same aliases held. No second Tuesday night. Heat behind the eyes didn’t come back.

Friday of week two: I closed the shop tablet at 6:05 p.m. and left without opening Excel. PackMonth is that boring streak — not “synergy,” just the quiet after the mess when the numbers finally sit still for more than one morning.
