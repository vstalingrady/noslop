Found a kid's shoe under a food-court table a little after 1 a.m. Left one. Blue. I almost stepped on it.

Radio was quiet. No tickets about a missing child. I put it in a bag and stuck it on the lost-and-found shelf next to some glasses.

Thought about calling the non-emergency line. Didn't. Walked the rest of the route instead. Loading dock was empty except for a cart that only rolls in circles.

Next night the shoe was gone. I didn't ask who took it. Finished the shift. The tile under that table was sticky again — same as always.
